Why it helps

The Rosary should draw you into prayer—not make you worry about what comes next.

Beadlight is made for the ordinary moments when prayer needs a little structure: when you lose your place, forget the next prayer, become distracted, or are still learning the rhythm of the Rosary.

It is also useful when you are returning to prayer, or when you want to pray while walking, resting or travelling.

Beadlight handles the structure, so you can focus on the prayer.

FAQ

Questions before you begin.

Is Beadlight free?

Yes. The core Rosary experience is free to begin, including the complete Rosary sequence, mysteries and bead-by-bead guidance. Guided Audio is an optional in-app purchase.

Do I need to create an account?

No. Beadlight can be used without an account. If you sign in, Beadlight may save your prayer journey so you can see personal streaks, totals and progress.

Is Beadlight an official Catholic Church app?

No. Beadlight is an independent Catholic Rosary app. It is not an official app of the Catholic Church.

Can I use a physical Rosary with Beadlight?

Yes. Beadlight is not intended to replace a physical Rosary. It can provide gentle guidance alongside physical beads whenever that is useful.

Does Beadlight listen to or record my prayers?

No. Beadlight does not record your voice, listen to your prayers or access your microphone for prayer tracking.

Which mysteries are included?

Beadlight includes the Joyful, Luminous, Sorrowful and Glorious Mysteries.

Can I use guided audio without looking at the screen?

Yes. Guided Audio and hands-free progression are designed to help you continue praying without repeatedly touching the screen.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Manage or cancel subscriptions through your App Store subscription settings. Account deletion does not automatically cancel an App Store subscription.

Is Beadlight available on Android?

Yes. Beadlight is available on the App Store for iPhone and Google Play for Android.

How can I request a feature?

Send a feature request through the support page, or review the public roadmap to see what is already planned or under consideration.
